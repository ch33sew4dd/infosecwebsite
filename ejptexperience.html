The eJPT Experience. [ 2019-9-8 ] /index/about-me

I started eJPT as a way to get familiar with how infosec courses work berfore I dove into PWK. I had already had put some time into Hack The Box and all the other '1337 h4x0r' services like vulnhub so I was somewhat familiar the pentesting process, I just didn't have any certifications or a 'methodology' as the cool kids say. In all reality I feel eJPT is actually a good learning experience and would definitely recommend it to anyone aspiring to get into the infosec industry.

I ended up doing pretty well on the exam despite rushing through the material in around 2 weeks. If I rememeber correctly I ended up with 23 out of 30 hours of lab time left before I took the leap towards the exam - Yeah. I didn't spend much time in the labs at all. My major weakpoint in the course itself was the C++ exploitation. You are tasked with writing a keylogger which seems easy right? I thought so too, but little did I know this would take me a solid 2 days of C++ learning.

The majority of my lab time was spent on the XSS and SQL Injection labs. I had an absolute blast playing with these two vulnerabilities. How cool is it that you can use input fields to exploit a website without having any other tools? It's just you, your keyboard, and the webdev's incompetency.

Is 400$ really a good price for eJPT? Absolutely. Sure it spoonfeeds you information, which is something that i'm not too fond of; but on the other hand if you're just getting started and have no idea how the pentesting process works this is definitely the course for you. If you want a springboard for getting the basics down before jumping into the OSCP exam process i'd recommend this course too.
